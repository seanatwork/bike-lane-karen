# CapMetro Bikeshare — Research Notes

## Public Datasets & Dashboards

- **[Austin MetroBike Trips](https://data.austintexas.gov)** — Trip duration, start/end stations, membership type. Available as CSV/JSON/API.
  - **⚠️ Data gap:** The 2024 vendor transition disrupted the data pipeline. As of late 2025, the city is working with CapMetro to restore the refresh — recent trip data is not yet available.
- **[Austin MetroBike Kiosk Locations](https://data.austintexas.gov)** — Lat/lon for all bike rental kiosks.
- **Real-time station status** — Available via the CityBikes API (includes CapMetro's network).
- **[Ride Report public dashboard](https://public.ridereport.com/austin)** — Best bet for up-to-date aggregated usage stats; linked from the city's official data portal.

## Key Performance Metrics

- **Growth:** Fewer than 5,000 monthly trips pre-2019 → ~28,000 by April 2023.
- **Transition disruption:** Annual ridership dipped from 316,980 (2022) to 283,965 (2023).
- **Typical trip (2024):** Median ride length ~8 minutes.

## Program Costs & Financing

- **2024 revamp:** Funded by $11.3M federal grant + $2.8M city match.
- **10-year operating contract** with CapMetro valued at up to **$18.55 million**.
- **Operating deficit:** CapMetro has stated fare revenue will not cover full operating costs.
- **FY2024 budget:** $2.4M allocated specifically for Bikeshare operations and expansion.
- **Rough capital cost per trip:** ~$6+/trip before operating expenses or fare revenue offset.

## History & User Experience

- **2020:** CapMetro took over Austin B-cycle, rebranded as MetroBike.
- **July 2024:** System shut down to transition to a 100% electric fleet under "CapMetro Bikeshare." Expansion goal: 76 stations → 300+ over the next decade.
- **App issues:** New app launched with major complaints — users couldn't add payment cards or purchase passes. Aggregate rating: **2.1–2.7 / 5 stars** across platforms.
- The app problems coincide with the data void, making it hard to track program performance from any angle.
