🌍 How does Austin compare to global safety standards?

Chlorophyll-a above 25 µg/L is where regulators around the world start issuing public health advisories. Austin's lake is well under that right now.

Standard Advisory trigger What it means
🇪🇺 EU Bathing Water Directive ~25 µg/L Post a visible-bloom advisory; test for cyanobacteria toxins before allowing swimming
🇺🇸 California (CalEPA) 25 µg/L State's strictest in the US — triggers sampling for microcystin toxin; beach closure possible at 50 µg/L
🌐 WHO Recreational Water 10 µg/L Low-risk threshold; monitoring recommended above this level
🤠 Austin / Texas varies TCEQ issues advisories based on visible bloom + cyanotoxin testing, not a fixed chlorophyll-a number

Lady Bird Lake's Oct 2025 peak (9.8 µg/L) fell below the WHO low-risk threshold. A level between 10–25 µg/L would warrant closer monitoring; above 25 µg/L would trigger a public advisory in California and most of Europe.

🐕 What to watch for in Austin: Lady Bird Lake sees its worst algae in late summer and early fall when the water is warm and slow-moving. Blue-green algae (cyanobacteria) looks like a green paint spill or pea soup on the surface. Keep pets out of any water with a visible scum — cyanotoxins can kill a dog within hours of exposure. Austin Parks & Recreation posts advisories at park entrances and on the city website when blooms are detected.

Data: Austin Water Quality Sampling dataset (chlorophyll-a, Lady Bird Lake watershed). µg/L = micrograms per liter, a standard unit for algae pigment concentration.
